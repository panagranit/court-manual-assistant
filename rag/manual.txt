SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION

Purpose of This Manual
This manual provides comprehensive guidance for court clerk staff in the performance of their duties. It establishes standardized procedures to ensure efficient, accurate, and professional court operations.

Role of the Court Clerk
The court clerk serves as the administrative officer of the court, responsible for maintaining official records, processing documents, managing case files, and facilitating court proceedings. The clerk acts as the liaison between the court, attorneys, litigants, and the public.

SECTION 2: FILING PROCEDURES

2.1 Document Filing Requirements
All documents filed with the court must:
- Include a case number (if applicable)
- Bear original signatures or electronic signatures as authorized
- Include proper caption and case style
- Comply with formatting requirements (font size, margins, page limits)
- Include proof of service when required
- Be accompanied by the appropriate filing fee

2.2 Initial Case Filing
When a new case is initiated:
1. Review the petition or complaint for completeness
2. Collect the filing fee or process fee waiver request
3. Assign a case number using the established numbering system
4. Create a case file with all required components
5. Enter case information into the case management system
6. Generate and issue summons or notice as required
7. Notify all relevant parties and departments

2.3 Subsequent Filings
For documents filed in existing cases:
1. Verify the case number and party information
2. Confirm the filing is timely or process any motion for late filing
3. Time-stamp the document upon receipt
4. Add the document to the case file
5. Update the case management system
6. Notify relevant parties if required

SECTION 3: CASE MANAGEMENT

3.1 Case Assignment and Tracking
All cases must be assigned to appropriate judicial officers within 24 hours of filing. The clerk shall:
- Maintain accurate case assignment records
- Monitor case progress and deadlines
- Coordinate with judicial officers regarding case status
- Ensure timely calendar entries

3.2 Case File Organization
Case files must be organized chronologically with:
- Original complaint/petition
- All filed documents in date order
- Court orders and judgments
- Correspondence and notices
- Financial records

SECTION 4: DOCUMENT PROCESSING

4.1 Document Preparation
The clerk shall ensure all court documents:
- Contain proper case captions
- Include accurate party information
- Bear appropriate signatures
- Comply with formatting standards
- Are served on all required parties

4.2 Document Authentication
Official court documents require:
- Clerk's signature and seal
- Date of authentication
- Proper certification language

SECTION 5: COURT SCHEDULING

5.1 Calendar Management
The clerk is responsible for:
- Maintaining court calendars
- Scheduling hearings and trials
- Coordinating with judicial officers
- Notifying parties of scheduled events
- Managing calendar conflicts

5.2 Continuances and Adjournments
Requests for continuances must:
- Be submitted in writing or on the record
- State valid reasons for the request
- Be approved by the judicial officer
- Be promptly communicated to all parties

SECTION 6: RECORD KEEPING

6.1 File Maintenance
All case files must be:
- Properly indexed and organized
- Stored in secure locations
- Accessible to authorized personnel
- Maintained for required retention periods

6.2 Record Security
Confidential records shall be:
- Stored in locked cabinets
- Accessed only by authorized personnel
- Protected from unauthorized disclosure
- Maintained in accordance with privacy laws

SECTION 7: PUBLIC ACCESS

7.1 Public Records
The following records are available to the public:
- Filed court documents (except sealed records)
- Court calendars and schedules
- Case disposition information
- Court rules and procedures

7.2 Record Requests
Requests for copies of public records shall be:
- Processed within established timeframes
- Accompanied by appropriate fees
- Provided in accordance with access policies

SECTION 8: FINANCIAL TRANSACTIONS

8.1 Fee Collection
Filing fees and other court costs must be:
- Collected at the time of filing
- Recorded accurately in financial systems
- Deposited promptly per established procedures
- Documented with proper receipts

8.2 Fee Waivers
Requests for fee waivers shall be:
- Reviewed for eligibility requirements
- Processed within established timeframes
- Approved or denied with written explanation
- Appealed through proper procedures if denied

SECTION 9: ETHICS AND CONDUCT

9.1 Professional Standards
Court clerks must maintain:
- Impartiality and fairness
- Professional demeanor
- Confidentiality of sensitive information
- Compliance with all applicable laws and rules

9.2 Conflict of Interest
Clerks shall avoid:
- Personal involvement in cases
- Financial interests in case outcomes
- Relationships that could impair impartiality
- Acceptance of gifts or favors from parties

SECTION 10: EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

10.1 Court Security Emergencies
In case of security threats or disturbances:
1. Immediately notify court security personnel
2. Clear the courtroom if necessary
3. Assist in evacuation procedures
4. Secure all case files and documents
5. Follow established emergency protocols
6. Document incident for official records

10.2 Medical Emergencies
When medical assistance is needed:
1. Call emergency services (911)
2. Provide location and nature of emergency
3. Assist medical personnel upon arrival
4. Notify appropriate court officials
5. Document incident and response
6. Follow up on medical emergency procedures

10.3 Building Evacuation
During building evacuation:
1. Follow established evacuation routes
2. Assist individuals with disabilities
3. Account for all court personnel
4. Secure sensitive documents if time permits
5. Report to designated assembly areas
6. Await instructions from emergency personnel

10.4 Severe Weather Procedures
During severe weather events:
1. Monitor weather alerts and warnings
2. Prepare for potential court closures
3. Notify judicial officers and staff
4. Post closure notices if applicable
5. Reschedule affected court proceedings
6. Document weather-related cancellations

10.5 System Failures
In case of computer or system failures:
1. Notify IT support immediately
2. Implement manual backup procedures
3. Maintain continuity of court operations
4. Document affected systems and duration
5. Coordinate with backup facilities if needed
6. Restore systems and verify functionality

10.6 Active Shooter Situations
During active shooter incidents:
1. Run, Hide, Fight as appropriate
2. Follow law enforcement instructions
3. Barricade doors if hiding
4. Silence all electronic devices
5. Do not sound fire alarms
6. Account for personnel after resolution

10.7 Bomb Threats
Upon receiving bomb threats:
1. Remain calm and note details of threat
2. Immediately notify security and law enforcement
3. Evacuate building per established procedures
4. Do not use cell phones or radios in building
5. Await instructions from bomb squad
6. Document threat details for investigation

10.8 Pandemic/Health Emergencies
During health emergencies:
1. Follow CDC and local health guidelines
2. Implement social distancing measures
3. Provide sanitization stations
4. Allow remote appearances when possible
5. Monitor staff and public health status
6. Coordinate with health authorities

10.9 Utility Failures
In case of power or utility outages:
1. Notify facilities management
2. Implement emergency lighting procedures
3. Preserve perishable records if applicable
4. Continue operations with manual procedures
5. Monitor for restoration of services
6. Document duration and impact

10.10 Post-Incident Procedures
After any emergency:
1. Conduct debriefing with involved personnel
2. Document incident and response actions
3. Review emergency procedures for improvements
4. Provide support to affected individuals
5. Update emergency contact information
6. Report incident to appropriate authorities
